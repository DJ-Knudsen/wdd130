Client Report:

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

It was a personal thing to me. It was all about my favorite book series, so I just needed to find the images of the first book of each series as well as of the authors, and the content was all just my opinions and recommendations.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

I talked to my developer directly, outside of emailing him my site plan. My site plan was pretty straight forward so I didn't hear back from him until he emailed me the link to my site.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

For the most part they got the information I wanted on there. However, there were definitely discrepencies. By the time the lead developer sent me the link to my site the project was pretty much over, so I didn't want to nitpick. However, they weren't on the same page for the CSS it seems, and the header, navigation, and footer weren't all the same. But they did get the information on there.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

For one thing, I will definitely get ahold of the website sooner rather than later. If it wasn't for the fact that i was already so late I would have asked them to follow the wireframe better. I'm a bit disappointed in how it turned out, truth be told, but oh well. It definitely opens my eyes to how it can be difficult as a client.

Lead Developer Report:

How did you manage your development team and communicate the tasks for your junior devs?

I made sure my junior developers all knew which pages they were in charge of. I uploaded the site plan from the client into the folder so everybody had access to it, and they did a pretty good job getting their parts done.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

Luckily my client sat right next to me, so every class I would show her the website from my laptop. She emailed me at one point and asked me to add an input box section for people to upload their own recommendations for anime. She was open about what she wanted, and she seemed pleased with the home page of the website at the very least.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?

Keeping the github up to date was pretty easy and straight forward. I emailed her the url for the website pretty early on. She had access to the website for most of the time that we were working on it.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

It wasn't bad! I think GitHub is a great resource, and I enjoy the fact that it's a free and easy way to create a public domain.

Junior Developer Report

How did you communicate with your team lead? Was there any issues?

I went up to both of my lead developers to make sure which page I was going to be working on. Early on they were able to tell me exactly which pages to work on, so I was able to get started on them when I needed to. There weren't really any issues with my lead developers.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?

Yep! Like I said, I talked to my lead developers early on and was able to know exactly which pages I were to work on. I had access to the right wireframes for both, and at least for one site I had all the content I needed. For the website that involved trails and national forests, we didn't receive any content pretty much. We got the wireframes and that's it, no information on any of the trails or sites he wanted.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?

Yes, I did my best to keep my sites as close to the wireframes as possible. I uploaded the content that I had to where it needed to go, and I was able to supply the content I needed for the site that I didn't have content to use.

Did you run into any issues with GitHub and adding your changes to the sites?

No, there was no issues with GitHub or adding my changes. Everything seemed to work smoothly!

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

Honestly the most challenging aspect was the site where we had no content given to us. I wasn't able to talk to the client, and my lead developer just said to include whatever content I wanted as long as it fit the theme for the site. If I ran into issues with how to do the code I would just look up how to do it on w3schools are goodle or some other way.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

Honestly it was fun seeing how everything turned out in the end. Being able to see a wireframe and being able to develop a website based on that wireframe and it actually looking like it was really fun.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I would try to get in contact with each of the clients, regardless whether I was the lead or junior developer. I think getting a clear image from each of the clients would help out a lot, which I was able to see with the website I was the lead developer on. Having direct input from the client helped immensely.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

I think yes, I am. This gave me insight in how working with others on a project like this goes, both as a client and as a lead or junior developer. I can see how lack of information can be frustrating, and why having that communication is so important. It was definitely eye-opening.
